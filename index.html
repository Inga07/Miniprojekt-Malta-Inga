Valletta

capital city

Valletta is the capital city of Malta, located on the island’s southern coast. Known for its rich history, the city is a UNESCO World Heritage site, featuring stunning baroque architecture, cobbled streets, and historic landmarks. Valletta is home to the impressive St. John’s Co-Cathedral, the Grand Master's Palace, and the National Museum of Archaeology. With its beautiful harbor views and vibrant cultural scene, Valletta offers a blend of history, art, and Mediterranean charm.

Mdina

the silent city

Mdina, also known as the "Silent City," is a historic fortified town in the central region of Malta. Known for its narrow, winding streets and stunning medieval and baroque architecture, Mdina served as the island's capital for centuries. The city offers panoramic views of Malta's countryside and is home to landmarks like St. Paul's Cathedral. With its tranquil atmosphere, Mdina exudes a timeless charm, making it a popular destination for visitors seeking a glimpse of Malta's past.

Golden Bay

perfect place to relax or climb up the cliffs

Golden Bay is a beautiful sandy beach located on the northwest coast of Malta. Known for its clear turquoise waters and scenic surroundings, it’s one of the island's most popular beach destinations. The bay is framed by dramatic cliffs and offers a peaceful atmosphere, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the natural beauty of Malta. Golden Bay is also home to several resorts and dining options, making it a great spot for relaxation and leisure.

Popeye Village

an old film set

Popeye Village, located in Anchor Bay, Malta, is a unique theme park built around the set of the 1980 film "Popeye", starring Robin Williams. Originally created as a film set, it has been preserved and transformed into a family-friendly attraction. Visitors can explore the colorful, rustic buildings, enjoy live shows, and participate in various activities. Surrounded by stunning coastal views, Popeye Village offers a fun and nostalgic experience for all ages.

Marsaxlokk

weekly fish market

Marsaxlokk is a picturesque fishing village located on the southeastern coast of Malta. Known for its vibrant harbor, colorful "luzzu" fishing boats, and traditional Mediterranean charm, the village offers a glimpse into Malta’s maritime heritage. The bustling Sunday fish market is a major draw, where visitors can enjoy fresh seafood and local crafts. Marsaxlokk is also home to several quaint restaurants with waterfront views, making it a perfect spot to relax and enjoy the island’s coastal beauty.

Dingli Cliffs

a perfect place to watch the sunsets

Dingli Cliffs is a stunning natural landmark located on the western coast of Malta. Rising 253 meters above sea level, these cliffs offer breathtaking panoramic views of the Mediterranean Sea and the nearby island of Filfla. The area is a popular spot for hiking, photography, and enjoying Malta's scenic beauty. The cliffs are also home to the small 17th-century chapel of St. Mary Magdalene, adding a touch of history to the striking landscape.
